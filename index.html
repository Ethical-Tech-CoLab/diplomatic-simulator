Why Nova / Statement of Interest

Today's policymakers are being asked to govern systems that were never designed in a lab, they evolve in the wild, settle value autonomously, and increasingly negotiate on behalf of humans. ETSAGP's leadership has named this gap directly: test-and-evaluation frameworks haven't kept up; we lack a vocabulary for governing systems whose internals are not yet legible.1

I sit inside that gap as a builder. As Microsoft's Director of Traceability and Cofounder of Blockchain, I lead production-grade programs on supply-chain traceability, agentic commerce rails (x402), and verifiable digital-twin systems (DTFS), the information-science substrate McQuade has flagged as central to ETSAGP's mandate.2 As a Professor of Global Affairs at NYU SPS Center for Global Affairs, I translate that work into a 14-week graduate course on Applied AI and the Human Condition, anchored by the Ethical Tech Summit and the Ethical Tech CoLab.

What I bring to the inaugural Nova cohort is not a research agenda dressed up as practice, it is the inverse: deployed systems, ready to be stress-tested, primed, and translated into policy artifacts the Belfer Center can publish, convene around, and teach from. I am unusually well-positioned to (a) operationalize Move37, (b) seed an AI + Commerce + Governance track inside AI+X+G, and (c) accelerate ETSAGP's Year-2 expansion into critical supply chains.3
