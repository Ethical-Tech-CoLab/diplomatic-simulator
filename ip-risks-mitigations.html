Microsoft IP, conflicts, and disclosure.

The proposed working paper, reference architecture, and convening have been scoped to respect Microsoft's IP, public-disclosure, and conflict-of-commitment obligations. Approximately 70% of the working paper is publishable as-is from public Microsoft references, EEA / x402 Foundation materials, and existing academic literature; the remaining material that touches Microsoft Traceability Program internals will be treated synthetically or routed through CELA and Microsoft Communications review on a published timeline. The Diplomacy Table reference architecture is built on the open-source DTFS / TwinForge stack and is publishable in full. I will comply with Harvard's conflict-of-interest, conflict-of-commitment, IP, and public-disclosure policies as required by the Nova program.

Risks & mitigations.

  • Microsoft IP review timing. Mitigation: parallel-track the publishable 70% of the working paper while CELA reviews the remaining 30%; have a synthetic-case fallback ready.
  • Move37 first-paper authorship circle already closed. Mitigation: the Diplomacy Table deliverable is positioned as a complementary reference architecture and demo, not a competing paper, and is co-publishable as an ETSAGP working paper independently.
  • Microsoft re-org affecting the Traceability Program mid-term. Mitigation: case-study material is already documented through completed program milestones; convening and working-paper outputs do not depend on continued day-to-day program access.
