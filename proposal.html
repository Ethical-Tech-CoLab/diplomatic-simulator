Nova Fellowship · Proposed Area of Focus & Output

Trustworthy Agentic Systems for Geopolitics

Verifiable Compute · Agentic Commerce · AI-Mediated Diplomacy
~470 words

Problem.

Three families of emerging technology are converging faster than the policy frameworks meant to govern them: (1) AI agents that negotiate and transact autonomously; (2) blockchain-based payment and attestation rails, notably the x402 protocol, that settle value at machine speed and machine granularity; and (3) digital-twin systems whose simulation outputs inform high-stakes public and private decisions. ETSAGP's leadership has named the underlying gap: there is no shared T&E vocabulary for systems designed to evolve in the wild, and no shared information-science framing across blockchain, encryption, and AI for the policymakers who must govern them.

Framing.

Treating these technologies as information-science primitives, rather than as products or sectors, yields a durable governance vocabulary, a T&E roadmap, and a bridge to live ETSAGP initiatives (Move37, AI+X+G, the Year-2 critical-supply-chains workstream). This fellowship will test that claim through three artifacts, each designed to generalize across blockchain, AI-agent, and digital-twin governance.

Deliverables, one arc, three artifacts.

  1. Policy primer / working paper, "Information-Science Foundations for Emerging-Tech Governance." ~8,000 words; co-publishable as an ETSAGP working paper. Names blockchain, verifiable compute, and agentic-commerce rails as information-science primitives; maps each to existing T&E, attestation, and disclosure regimes; identifies the highest-leverage policy interventions for the next 24 months.
  2. Move37-aligned reference architecture + working demo, "From Diplomacy Table to Doctrine." A documented reference architecture for AI-augmented multilateral negotiation integrating linguistic and non-linguistic agents, with a working demo for HKS students and Move37 collaborators, including a five-round multi-team simulation of the 2026 Iran–US Strait of Hormuz crisis already run on the platform. Includes guardrails, provenance, and auditability patterns transferable to mediation, treaty drafting, and crisis communications. Live demo: Diplomacy Table Session Report.
  3. Convening, "Critical Supply Chains in the Agentic Era." A one-day workshop co-hosted with the Year-2 supply-chain workstream and AI+X+G; ~30 participants from Microsoft, NYU, HKS, Coinbase, and US federal practitioners (UFLPA/NDAA). Output: a structured policy-recommendations memo plus a published convening summary.

Method.

I will work primarily out of my Microsoft and NYU programs of record, using the Microsoft Company-wide Traceability Program, the Supplychain Graph, DTFS, and the Diplomacy Table as live case material, and translate them into publishable policy artifacts in collaboration with Move37 leads, AI+X+G fellows, the supply-chain workstream, and a designated faculty co-author where appropriate. Preliminary scoping conversations with members of the Move37 working group and AI+X+G fellows could begin upon acceptance to inform the deliverables proposed above.

Term & Cambridge presence.

Six months part-time, September 2026 – February 2027. Approximately two weeks per month on campus during October, November, and January, exceeding the 30% on-campus requirement. New York-to-Cambridge logistics are manageable; my employer is supportive.

Success criteria.

  • Working paper accepted into the ETSAGP publication stream.
  • Diplomacy Table demo run for at least one HKS course or Move37 working session.
  • Convening held with ≥30 cross-sector participants and a published recommendations memo.
  • Clean handoff to ETSAGP staff and Tech & Geopolitics Fellows so the work continues after the term.
