# Cyprus Simulation — Cross-Party Contamination Audit

## 1) Information Leakage
Strikingly little. The most sensitive SECRETs never surface, which is the strongest evidence *against* bleeding:
- **TRNC's** private fear that "immigrants now outnumber native Turkish Cypriots and threaten Turkish Cypriot identity" — never referenced by anyone, including Turkey, whose public line (R1: "the status quo has entrenched Greek Cypriot dominance") stays generic.
- **Turkey's** true goal ("Ultimate goal is a strong, independent TRNC; recognition first, not genuine unification") — never named. GC-side rejection of "two-state" (CY R2, GR R2) is legitimate inference from TRNC/TR's *public* "two equal founding states" (R1), not leakage.
- **Greece's** self-interested motive (secretly favors Türkiye's EU accession "because it would benefit the Greek economy") — Greece only ever frames de-linking as "confidence-building" (R1/R2); the motive stays hidden.
- **CY's** census-as-war-crime plan (Fourth Geneva Convention framing for repatriation) — never voiced; CY pushes only a neutral "UN-supervised whole-island census."

**Borderline item:** US proposes intervention "confined to a constituent state, not a veto over the whole island" (R2, R3). Greece's SECRET fallback is intervention "strictly limited to constituent-state boundaries." Greece never stated this publicly, so US's identical framing is suggestive — but constituent-state limitation is a standard federal-compromise concept and TR echoes it too. **Verdict: convergent design, not confirmed bleeding. Low severity.**

Net: no confirmed true-bleeding instance.

## 2) Convergence
Realistically frictional. Red lines held on both sides through R3: GC bloc keeps "single international personality"; TRNC/TR keep "two founding states." The **sovereignty gap is never closed** (US R3: "the gap that persists is sovereignty"). Convergence is confined to CBMs (Famagusta, Ercan, DTR, de-linking, UN-framed treaty) that each party independently held in its own instructions. No fake deal.

## 3) Stylistic Homogenization — HIGH
One authorial hand is obvious:
- **"real convergence"** appears in R3 from CY, GR, TR, UK, *and* US.
- Uniform template every round: "red lines held" → symmetric "if X then we will actively champion Y" → "Way forward:".
- Shared openings ("Mr. Special Adviser"), shared tics ("We coordinate fully with Türkiye" — TRNC & TR).

## 4) Single-Model Effect
Heavy on *form*, light on *content*. The one model imposed a shared rhetorical skeleton and a symmetric bargaining grammar, and steered all sides to the same CBM bundle. But it kept red lines distinct and — critically — kept each delegation's hidden agenda hidden.

## OVERALL VERDICT: **LOW** bleeding
Trust the substantive positions, red-line integrity, and the unresolved sovereignty gap — these are clean. Discount the *style* (voices are near-identical) and treat the neat CBM dovetailing and the shared "constituent-state intervention" phrasing as single-model convergence artifacts rather than genuine independent agreement.
