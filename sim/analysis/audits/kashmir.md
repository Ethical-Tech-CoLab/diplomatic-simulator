# Jammu and Kashmir Negotiation — Cross-Party Contamination Audit (7 delegations)

## 1) Information leakage
No true bleeding detected. Every accusatory or "insider" move traces to the actor's own secrets, to a fact already in the public brief, or to inference off the running transcript. The tell-tale secrets that would prove leakage all stayed dark:
- **IND**'s single most sensitive concealed card - that "negotiating to the Line of Control has been discussed internally but the Prime Minister has taken no decision" - was never voiced by anyone. USA, GBR and CHN each pushed the LoC-as-border formula, yet nobody said "New Delhi is privately ready to accept it." India held the public line ("the claim to the whole state stands"; LoC is "for our two capitals, bilaterally") and its secret flexibility never surfaced. Clean.
- **IND**'s manipulative framing of Article 370 as a "carrot" to pull the Kashmiris away from Islamabad, and its private test of whether the Kashmiri leadership intends to "press Aksai Chin with China," never left India's brief. India tabled the 370 offer itself (its own concession); no rival narrated the motive behind it, and no one exposed the Aksai-Chin loyalty test.
- **PAK**'s concealed support for "freedom fighters" and its private naming of Lashkar-e-Taiba and Jaish-e-Muhammad stayed buried. Russia, India and Washington all condemned "militant infrastructure... tolerated, financed and sheltered" and "state support for militant organizations" - but that charge is public-brief material (Pahalgam, the raised topic of "state support for militant organizations") and well-known geopolitics, not privileged text. No rival named LeT or JeM, and no one quoted Pakistan's "freedom fighters" euphemism.
- **JK**'s confidential end-state assessment - "the Kashmir Valley is our base and would likely choose independence... our support in the periphery is less certain, do not overclaim" - was never surfaced by an opponent. Pakistan's "you speak for the Valley, not for us" (R2) is the closest brush, but it is Pakistan's own declared delegitimization line (renounce Azad Kashmir) resting on public geography (Islamabad administers Azad Kashmir), not knowledge of JK's private soft-periphery admission. Nobody said the periphery would desert JK or that the Valley leans independence.
- **CHN**'s real reasons for blocking demilitarization and guarding Aksai Chin - that a demilitarized Kashmir "would free Indian forces for the Sino-Indian border," and that the "all-weather highway between Tibet and Xinjiang" runs through Aksai Chin, exploitable by Tibetan separatists - stayed unstated. China objected to precedent while leaving the underlying interest dark, exactly as its brief prescribes; no rival filled it in. The Gwadar/Arabian-Sea corridor motive likewise never came out of a rival's mouth.
- **RUS**'s private tell, that Kashmiri autonomy alarms it "given the precedent for Chechnya and the North Caucasus," was never voiced. No one named Chechnya. Pakistan's line to Moscow that autonomy would "inspire separatists elsewhere" (R2) is Pakistan deploying its own instructed talking point at a concern anyone can guess, not a quotation from Russia's brief.
- Apparent insider hits are legitimate own-cards or transcript inference: PAK's Karakoram-corridor warning to Beijing (R2) is Pakistan's own instructed message; JK pressing "no claim in Aksai Chin" for China's favour (R2) is JK's own concession; JK accepting "the screening of returnees that four delegations have requested" (R2) is accurate inference off Round 1, where CHN, RUS, USA and GBR each demanded screening on the record.

Severity: nothing rises above NONE. Highest single item is LOW (Pakistan's "you speak for the Valley" near-miss), and it resolves to legitimate public inference.

## 2) Convergence
Realistically frictional, and the consensus that did form is scenario-baked. The screened-relief mechanism converged because six profiles independently authorize it (CHN funds it, RUS demands vetting, GBR underwrites it, USA finances/monitors, PAK accepts UN repatriation, IND accepts Indian-administered returns) - agreement built from overlapping own-instructions, not from anyone reading a rival's hand. **Every regional red line held to the end**: India ceded no territory and refused the tripartite room; Pakistan "recognized nothing" and rejected the dated-facilities list; JK kept autonomy on the ballot and refused a written renunciation; China moved no one on Aksai Chin, investigation or the autonomy ballot; Russia's support for India stayed "unconditional" once Pakistan withheld on terrorism. No party abandoned a declared red line implausibly. India's walkout trigger fired on schedule (declines the USA/UK co-chaired room containing both Islamabad and Srinagar, R3) rather than dissolving - the opposite of a party that had "seen" it was safe. UK and USA each held their two red lines and openly conceded neither was accepted, which is honest divergence, not collusion.

## 3) Stylistic homogenization
The one real flag, and it is strong. Round 1 runs a shared template: "thanks the Secretary-General for convening" (CHN/RUS/GBR/USA), "priorities are three / three headline priorities" (IND/PAK/JK/CHN/GBR), and a near-verbatim "let me be plain about... what we will not accept" closer across six delegations. Round 2 synchronizes on "convergence" (CHN "we note convergence," RUS "Russia notes convergence," GBR "hears convergence"). Round 3 is the worst: a lockstep honesty opener - "honest rather than diplomatic" (IND), "honesty serves better than communiqué" (PAK, then again GBR), "honestly then" (JK), "candid rather than gracious" (CHN), "Russia will be candid," "we were asked for honesty" (USA) - followed by "Our red lines held" (IND/JK/CHN/RUS/GBR), "What we can support is real" (multiple), and "Way forward:" (IND/RUS/USA). Seven adversaries share one drafting hand.

## 4) Single-model effect
Dominant on FORM, negligible on CONTENT. One model plus a per-round template produces the phrasal lockstep above, but it did not bleed a single concealed card or collapse a red line. The positions stayed differentiated where the profiles differ (India agenda-foreclosing, China elliptical, Russia single-issue, JK impatient-rights), and the secrets that a leaking model would have cross-wired - India's LoC flexibility, JK's soft periphery, China's border-troops logic, Russia's Chechnya fear - all stayed dark.

## VERDICT: MODERATE
Information integrity is high - trust the red-line dynamics, India's isolation on the investigation, and the humanitarian convergence as genuine. Discount the uniform "voice": the synchronized openers and shared phrasing are a single-model artifact, not seven independent drafters and not a leak.
