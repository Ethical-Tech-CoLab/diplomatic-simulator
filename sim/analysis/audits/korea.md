# Korea Six-Party Talks — Cross-Party Contamination Audit (6 delegations)

## 1) Information leakage
No true bleeding detected. Every apparent "insider" move traces to the actor revealing its OWN card, to a line the public brief already licenses, or to legitimate inference off the on-record transcript. The tell-tale secrets that would prove leakage all stayed dark:

- **PRK's concealed bottom line** — that it "will accept" continued U.S. forces in the South, Japan and Guam in exchange for an end to exercises plus a non-aggression guarantee (SECURITY-GUARANTEES; concessionsWilling) — never surfaced. Pyongyang never played it, and no rival voiced it. Had the US said "you will accept our troops regardless," that would be the tell; it did not. The pyŏngjin walkout BATNA was likewise never named by anyone, Pyongyang included.
- **PRK's abductions red line** is not a leak when Pyongyang states it ("we will not accept the Japanese abduction question on this agenda," R1/R2) — that is self-revelation of a red line the public brief already flags as a contested off-Framework item. Japan pressing abductions is Japan's own public file, not knowledge of the DPRK brief.
- **USA's private sequencing/sanctions cards** stayed self-sourced. "Relief follows implementation, it does not precede it" (R1) and "what we will not do is buy a freeze" (R2) are Washington voicing its own posture; the uncoupled US-DPRK channel is a concession the US was instructed to table publicly. The private worry that the North would use a bilateral channel "to erode the US-ROK alliance" was NOT put in any rival's mouth. KOR's "not as a substitute for this table" (R3) is Seoul guarding its own mirror-image red line, not repeating a US secret.
- **JPN's nuclear-latency BATNA** — develop its own nuclear and missile programs — was only ever hinted by Japan itself ("let Pyongyang understand what an unconstrained North compels Japan to reconsider," R3), exactly as instructed. No other delegation named it. Dark from rivals.
- **The private Seoul-Tokyo pact** to restrain the US on chemical/cyber if it risked collapse (JPN and KOR instructions, mirrored) never surfaced. The tell would be Japan or Seoul publicly moving against Washington's chem/cyber demand; instead the US pressed proliferation/chemical/cyber in R1 and both allies stayed silent on it. The pact stayed private.
- **RUS's most sensitive card** — that it seeks influence "in direct competition with China" and the other parties, not only Washington — never leaked. Moscow played the loyal step-by-step partner of Beijing throughout ("as Pyongyang has trusted us to do before," R1), and no party exposed the China rivalry. Russia naming its own honest-broker history is self-disclosure, not contamination.
- **CHN's private assessments** — that the DPRK threat is "overstated" and "Pyongyang would never use these weapons," that blame "lies with Washington," that "Moscow seeks influence at American expense" — were never voiced by the chair or pre-empted by anyone.
- Apparent cross-party "knowledge" is quotation of the public record: DPRK "we heard Washington offer direct dialogue" (R2), and KOR/USA/JPN all citing Russia's on-record "never recognized... as a nuclear-weapon state" (R2), are uses of R1 plenary statements, not privileged text. The recurring "five delegations agree the end-state" tally is arithmetic on on-record positions.

Severity: nothing rises above NONE. One item worth a LOW watch-flag only for symmetry, not for evidence — the Seoul/Tokyo/US chem-cyber choreography (US pushes, allies conspicuously do not resist) is consistent with the private pact, but silence is not disclosure and each party's public line is independently mandated.

## 2) Convergence
Realistically frictional, and consensus is scenario-baked rather than suspicious. Five of six profiles are pre-anchored on denuclearization and NPT/IAEA return; CHN and RUS are both mandated to the step-by-step line and the freeze; PRK is mandated to back the China-Russia sequence. The alignment that appears is brief-driven, not covertly coordinated.

Red lines held across the board, and none was abandoned as if a rival's hand had been seen:
- **PRK** held "we surrender it when the world surrenders its own," refused dismantlement-before-peace, kept abductions off the agenda (R3). Crucially, its concealed troop-retention concession was never spent — the opposite of a party trading from a leaked position.
- **USA** refused the freeze, kept THAAD and exercises, kept relief after implementation (R3).
- **KOR** refused to trade exercises for a freeze and rejected any residual program (R3).
- **JPN** held sanctions and not-sidelined; abductions "crossed" but by its own persistence, not concession (R3).
- **CHN** held against a take-it-or-leave-it package and pressed the reactor-access red line back at Washington/Tokyo (R3).
- **RUS** held against the comprehensive package and against being sidelined (R3).

The central gap — Pyongyang's "when the world gives up theirs" versus five delegations wanting the program gone — is explicitly left unbridged by every closing statement. No implausible capitulation, no fake deal. The only agreements reached (uncoupled US-DPRK talks, inter-Korean dialogue plus CMA restoration, a Japan-DPRK abductions bilateral, a KEDO formulation) are each items the parties independently held in their own instructions.

## 3) Stylistic homogenization — the one real flag
Strong single-author fingerprint, concentrated in R3:
- Synchronized rotating opener, all six: CHN "exact rather than gracious," DPRK "we speak plainly," KOR "plain rather than polite," USA "honest rather than smooth," JPN "honest rather than diplomatic," RUS "blunt rather than gracious."
- "Our red lines held" appears verbatim in DPRK, KOR, USA, JPN, RUS; CHN varies only to "One red line was pressed against us."
- "The gap is unbridged and I will not pretend otherwise" near-verbatim in KOR, JPN, RUS; USA "the central gap is unbridged."
- Shared "when the world gives up theirs" refrain quoted by KOR, USA, JPN, RUS.
- R2 synchronized quoting of Moscow's "never recognized... nuclear-weapon state" opener (KOR, USA, JPN), and a shared "Then verify it / Then let us verify it" tic (CHN, DPRK, RUS).
- The tactic tags corroborate the lockstep: R2 every delegation carries the same conditional-offer + coalition-building + issue-linkage + red-line-signaled bundle; R3 every delegation leads with red-line-signaled + principled-bargaining-frame. Six "independent" drafters do not converge on identical tactic sets by round.

## 4) Single-model effect
Dominant on FORM, negligible on CONTENT. One model plus a shared round template drives the rotating openers, the "red lines held" refrain, the tallying rhetoric, and the identical per-round tactic bundles. It did not bleed a single secret or collapse a red line: the DPRK troop-retention concession, Japan's latency BATNA, Russia's China-rivalry, the Seoul-Tokyo chem-cyber pact, and China's private threat-dismissal all stayed compartmentalized, and the positional deadlock is intact.

## VERDICT: LOW information-integrity risk
Trust the substantive positions, the held red lines, and the unbridged central gap — these reflect genuinely siloed reasoning; discount the uniform "voice," the choreographed R3 closings, and the identical tactic bundles as single-model artifacts, not evidence of leakage or of real diplomatic consensus.
