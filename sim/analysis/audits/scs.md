# SCS Negotiation — Cross-Party Contamination Audit (9 delegations)

## 1) Information leakage
No true bleeding detected. Every move traces to the actor's own secrets or to public inference. The tell-tale secrets that would prove leakage all stayed dark:
- **VNM** never had its Amphitrite-for-Crescent Paracels swap card surfaced by anyone; nor was its concealed economic dependence on China exploited. CHN (R2) lumped Vietnam generically with Manila, no trade-leverage probe.
- **IND**'s walkout trigger (Jammu-Kashmir/border) was never touched — CHN never raised it, so India never had to deploy its concealed red line. Clean.
- **CHN**'s own secret playbook (link SCS to Taiwan/Senkaku; pressure Japan on WWII history) was never voiced — and no rival pre-empted it.
- **MYS/IDN/BRN**'s shared private assessment that "Philippine claims fail UNCLOS land-formation tests" never leaked; all three publicly backed Manila.
- Apparent "insider" hits are legitimate public inference: CHN offering "Luconia" to Malaysia (R2) and "Natuna's waters" to Indonesia (R2) invokes famously public geography, not privileged text. MYS revealing it would "shelve Ambalat" (R2) is its *own* concession.

Severity: none rises above NONE/LOW.

## 2) Convergence
Realistically frictional. 8/9 profiles are pre-anchored on UNCLOS/2016 award, so consensus is scenario-baked, not suspicious. **CHN held every red line to the end** ("the award is null and non-binding… no rollback," R3) — no capitulation. Better still, the three broker states each *compete* for the chair rather than coordinate it away: MYS "best placed to carry it," BRN "the facilitator's chair," IDN "broker that China channel directly" — independent pursuit of conflicting private goals, the opposite of collusion.

## 3) Stylistic homogenization — the one real flag
Strong single-author fingerprint in R3: near-verbatim "Our red lines held" (all nine), "Way forward:", "keep every door open," "Eight delegations converge." R2 shares a synchronized "hears China's Nansha… offer" opener (MYS/BRN/IDN/IND/JPN). Voices are structurally identical even where interests diverge.

## 4) Single-model effect
Dominant on *form*, negligible on *content*. One model + a shared round template drives the phrasal lockstep, but it did not bleed secrets or collapse red lines.

## VERDICT: MODERATE
Information integrity is high — trust the positional/red-line dynamics and CHN's isolation. Discount the appearance of distinct "voices": the uniform diction is a single-model artifact, not nine independent drafters.
