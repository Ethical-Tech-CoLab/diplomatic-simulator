**United States**: We appreciate the straightforward positions tabled here. To Canada: we value our alliance and your Article 234 expertise. The 1988 Arctic Cooperation Agreement has served us well—but we cannot agree that the Northwest Passage becomes subject to formal transit notification when ice retreats. International straits remain international, and codifying notification sets a dangerous precedent elsewhere. We stand by freedom of navigation. To Russia: Article 234 does not transform the Northern Sea Route into territorial waters requiring military oversight of foreign vessels. That interpretation contradicts the treaty text and the law of the sea. To all partners, we propose this: if all Arctic states commit to a binding notification and transparency framework for military movements and exercises in the High North, the United States will accelerate its UNCLOS accession and anchor our security posture in that collective framework. We seek stability rooted in law, not assertion.
