To Russia: you call Article 234 a red line — freedom of navigation is ours. A non-party still holds you to customary law; the NSR is an international strait, not a tollbooth demanding 45-day warship notice. That will not stand. To Canada, our ally: your 1988 Arctic Cooperation Agreement proves my point — consent-based passage already works without recharacterizing the Passage as internal waters or notification. So our offer: settle Beaufort on equidistance and drop transit-notification for every vessel, and Washington will formally signal UNCLOS accession, broker your boundary disputes, and anchor a Western risk-reduction framework with Norway, Denmark, and Finland. To China: "near-Arctic" is no legal status, a Polar Silk Road tethered to Moscow is not stewardship, and neither Council membership nor any permanent military presence in the Arctic is on the table.
