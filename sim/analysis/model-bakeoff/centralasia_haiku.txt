Mr. Special Representative, Kazakhstan thanks Uzbekistan for proposing a concrete package linking water supply to mining expertise and commodity routes—this bilateral spirit of mutual gain reflects our de-escalation vision precisely. We confirm readiness to explore water-for-trade arrangements respecting each nation's resource sovereignty.

To Russia: we hear your offer of arms on favorable credit. However, let me be direct: EAEU expansion stands outside our negotiating mandate, and Astana's energy architecture flows from sovereign choice, not external conditions. We remain open to bilateral security dialogue.

To China: we reaffirm the Middle Corridor compact. If Beijing commits BRI financing and locks in Kyrgyz-Kazakh labor with your technical oversight, Kazakhstan immediately opens transit corridors and co-develops pipeline infrastructure—binding connectivity to prosperity for all.

Water sovereignty remains our firm red line. Kazakhstan brokers frameworks; we subordinate to none.
