Kazakhstan welcomes the convergence on status-quo demarcation, but two points need sharpening. To Russia: your offer of arms on credit for deeper EAEU energy integration is declined, again. Kazakhstan chooses its own architecture; resource sovereignty is not a bargaining chip, however favorable the terms — that red line does not move, and further pressure ends this bilateral channel. To Uzbekistan: we cannot "supply water" as your proposal implies — sovereignty over Kazakh water is absolute — but we table this instead: if Tashkent locks in reciprocal free-transit and lower tariffs across the Middle Corridor now, Astana pairs that with joint irrigation-technology and efficiency cooperation, no water export required. To China: our rail-branch offer stands; confirm financing and labor terms so framework becomes implementation.
