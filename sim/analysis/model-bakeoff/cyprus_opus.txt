To the United States: your formula, confining any residual intervention role to a constituent state and never a whole-island veto, is one we can build on. To the TRNC and Türkiye: you branded a popular-vote presidency "disenfranchisement." We reject that. A single sovereignty needs one legitimating mandate, and this red line holds. But we hear the equality concern, so here is our answer: if you accept a single international personality with a directly elected President, Cyprus will endorse the UK's cross-voting mechanism so Turkish Cypriot support is required to win, plus a Senate-elected Vice-President. In exchange, Türkiye commits to a verified, timelined troop-and-settler withdrawal, whereupon we lift our blocks on EU accession and Direct Trade and open Famagusta once owners are reinstated. Equality through cross-voting, not two states.
