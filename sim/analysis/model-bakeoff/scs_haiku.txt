To the Philippines: we understand your call for ASEAN unity, yet bilateral tracks with each state—not plural voices to Beijing—ensure durable deals. Here is our offer: if Manila accepts the Nansha Joint Resource Development Agency, ends provocative patrols in our claimed zones, and shelves the 2016 award, then China guarantees Scarborough barrier removal, coast-guard restraint, and binding joint-fisheries pacts yielding 40-percent Manila revenue share over ten years. To Vietnam: the same terms apply. Our ten-dashed-line sovereignty rests on history millennia old, not UNCLOS island definitions—this red line is non-negotiable. Yet within it lies prosperity for all. To Washington, Tokyo, Delhi: this sea belongs to Asians. Stand down, and let us build.
