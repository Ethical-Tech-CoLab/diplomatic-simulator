# ARCTIC DIALOGUE CONFERENCE — TRONDHEIM (Notional, Feb 2026)
UN-convened negotiation. A Special Representative of the Secretary-General on the Arctic (SRSG) chairs the plenary and mediates. Seven delegations: Canada, China, Denmark (Kingdom, incl. Greenland), Finland, Norway, Russia, United States. An actual complete resolution is not expected; each delegation pursues its own end-states.

## Current situation
Climate change has opened the Northern Sea Route (NSR, Russian coast) and Northwest Passage (NWP, Canadian archipelago) for longer seasons. Continental-shelf claims (Lomonosov Ridge: Russia, Canada, Denmark) overlap over the North Pole and are unsettled at the UN CLCS. Russia has re-militarized its Arctic and requires 45-day notice for warships on the NSR; Canada regulates NWP transit. The US (not a UNCLOS party) insists both are international straits (freedom of navigation) and defined its own extended continental shelf unilaterally in 2023. China, a self-declared "near-Arctic state," pursues a "Polar Silk Road" with Russia and an expanding icebreaker fleet. Sanctions have slowed Russian energy projects, deepening Russia-China alignment. The Arctic Council has been paralyzed since 2022; the region is fracturing into 7 NATO-aligned states vs. a Sino-Russian partnership. Indigenous peoples (Inuit, Sámi) seek inclusion.

## Negotiation issues on the table
1. TERRITORY — overlapping continental-shelf/ECS claims (Lomonosov Ridge); Svalbard EEZ vs. Svalbard Treaty equal-access dispute.
2. SEA-LANES — legal status of NWP & NSR: sovereign control (Canada/Russia, UNCLOS Art. 234) vs. international straits / transit passage (US, China).
3. MILITARIZATION — rising military presence; risk-reduction, notification, exercise transparency.
4. RESOURCES — oil/gas licensing, Arctic fisheries limits, state vs. private extraction.
5. ENVIRONMENT — warming 3-4x faster; emissions, protected areas, spill/SAR (search-and-rescue) capacity.
6. GOVERNANCE — future of the Arctic Council, UNCLOS applicability, observer roles (China), dispute mechanisms.
7. INDIGENOUS — rights, co-governance, inclusion of Inuit/Sámi in decisions.

## Procedure & rules
- Plenary-with-caucus. The SRSG may invite proposals and summarize.
- Statements are public (on the record). Delegations may signal coalitions, make conditional offers, and cite law/precedent.
- Delegations must protect their Privileged Instructions: never disclose your secret bottom lines or hidden goals verbatim; reveal only what serves your strategy.
