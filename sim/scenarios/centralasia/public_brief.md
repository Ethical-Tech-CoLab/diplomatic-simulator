# Central Asia Dialogue: The Fergana Valley Crisis

## Current situation

The Fergana Valley — a densely populated agricultural basin shared by Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, and Tajikistan — is one of Central Asia's most volatile regions, with roughly 110 kilometers of border still in dispute. The valley depends on the Syr Darya river system, whose upstream-downstream dynamics have repeatedly triggered armed clashes, most severely the September 2022 Kyrgyz-Tajik conflict that killed dozens and displaced well over 100,000 people. Water scarcity, energy emergencies, three consecutive years of drought, land degradation, and religious extremism compound the instability. Great powers are deeply entangled: Russia treats the region as its privileged "near abroad" through the CSTO, China has become the dominant economic and security partner via the BRI, and India pursues influence through security ties with Tajikistan. Existing bodies such as the ICWC and CSTO have proven unable to resolve inter-state water and border disputes. Concerned that these tensions could escalate into a wider crisis, the UN has convened this dialogue at UNRCCA headquarters in Ashgabat.

## Negotiation issues on the table

1. BORDERS — Delimit and demarcate the ~110 km of disputed Fergana Valley frontier, including exclaves like Vorukh.
2. WATER — Allocate transboundary Syr Darya/Amu Darya flows and reform the paralyzed ICWC consensus mechanism.
3. HYDROPOWER — Reconcile Tajikistan's Roghun Dam and Kyrgyz winter power needs with downstream water security.
4. ENERGY — Address Kyrgyzstan's energy emergency, drought-driven shortages, and nuclear-waste dam hazards.
5. SECURITY — Manage CSTO/SCO overlap and the absence of any mechanism for member-on-member conflict.
6. EXTREMISM — Coordinate counterterrorism against ISIS-K, IMU, and cross-border radicalization from Afghanistan.
7. CLIMATE — Adapt shared resource management to droughts, glacier melt, and desertification.
8. INFLUENCE — Balance Russian, Chinese, and Indian competition against Central Asian strategic autonomy.

## Procedure & rules

Sessions convene in plenary chaired by the UN Special Representative of the Secretary-General for Central Asia (SRSG). All statements delivered in plenary are public and on the record. Each delegation must safeguard its privileged instructions and disclose nothing beyond its authorized negotiating position.
