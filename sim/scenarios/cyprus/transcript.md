
## ROUND 1

**Republic of Cyprus**: Mr. Special Adviser, the Republic of Cyprus welcomes these talks and thanks the United Nations. Let us be clear from the outset: the Republic of Cyprus already exists, is a sovereign EU member state, and is internationally recognized. UN Security Council Resolution 541 declared the so-called TRNC legally invalid. Our goal is reunification: a single sovereignty, single citizenship, and single international personality within a bizonal, bicommunal federation governed by a strong central government under a popularly elected presidency. We reject any 'virgin birth' that dissolves our state into two equal entities. Our priorities are demilitarization, withdrawal of foreign troops and settlers, return of Varosha, and property justice for the displaced, all conforming to the EU acquis and human rights. We approach in good faith and seek partnership with Greece, our EU partners, the United Kingdom, and the United States. Peace is possible on lawful foundations.

**Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus**: The Turkish Cypriot delegation comes to this table constructively, but on one foundation: the political equality of two peoples. Half a century of failed federations, from the collapse of 2004 to Crans-Montana in 2017, teaches one lesson. No durable settlement can graft our community onto the existing Greek-Cypriot-run Republic. We therefore anchor on a genuinely new partnership of two equal founding states, a light central architecture confined to a handful of shared institutions, and the enduring security guarantees our people have relied upon since 1960. On the ground, we seek to end the economic isolation of Turkish Cypriots: implement the Direct Trade Regulation and open Famagusta under international supervision. We are prepared to discuss Varosha's redevelopment, property, and confidence measures once equality is affirmed. We coordinate closely with Turkiye and welcome the constructive engagement of the United Kingdom and United States.

**Greece**: Greece comes as guarantor of legality, not of division. Our starting point is settled law: UN Security Council Resolution 541 declared the entity in the north legally invalid, and the Republic of Cyprus, a sovereign UN and EU member, already exists with a single, continuous international personality. Any settlement must integrate the occupied areas into that Republic. We reject two-state 'sovereign equality' and the 'virgin birth' concept outright. Our anchors: a strong federal government with no rotating presidency, the full return of Varosha to its lawful owners, abolition of the anachronistic 1960 guarantee system, and full withdrawal of foreign forces on a defined timeline. Cyprus's sovereign rights in its EEZ stand apart from these talks. We defer to Nicosia on internal matters and stand with the Republic of Cyprus. In a constructive spirit, we would back Turkiye's EU path as genuine confidence-building.

**Turkey**: Türkiye comes to this table as a Guarantor Power and the indispensable protector of the Turkish Cypriot people, and we speak in full coordination with, and behind the lead of, the TRNC delegation. Let us be candid: the status quo has for decades entrenched Greek Cypriot political and economic dominance, and any durable settlement must correct that. Our headline priorities are three. First, political equality: a single nation is conceivable only if it rests on two strong, equal constituent states with a light central government, born fresh rather than inherited from a Republic that does not represent us. Second, security: the 1960 guarantee and alliance arrangements must be preserved absent an acceptable replacement. Third, an end to the unjust isolation of Turkish Cypriots, including direct trade. We favor practical steps, including generous redevelopment offers for Varosha and opening Famagusta port under international supervision. We look to the UK and US as constructive partners. We negotiate in good faith, but not from weakness.

**United Kingdom**: The United Kingdom comes to this table as a friend of all Cypriots and a steadfast supporter of the UN Special Adviser, whose mediation must remain the single track we all reinforce. Our headline priority is clear: a settlement that produces one Cyprus, with a single sovereignty, a single citizenship, and a single international personality, grounded in Security Council resolutions. Within that anchor we are pragmatic and creative. On governance we will explore weighted or cross-voting so both communities feel genuinely secure. On Varosha and property we favour arrangements that compensate dispossessed owners while respecting present investment. On security we back replacing the 1960 treaties with a modern UN-framed arrangement. We are ready to help unlock economic ties and de-link Turkiye's European path from this dispute. We look forward to working closely with the United States, the SASG, and every delegation acting in good faith.

**United States**: The United States comes to this table as a committed friend of the process and of the Special Adviser. Our headline priority is clear: the status quo of permanent division is unacceptable, and the goal remains a single, reunified Republic of Cyprus, a bizonal, bicommunal federation with one international personality and a government strong enough to speak for the whole island. We anchor on that framework because UN resolutions and decades of mediation point to no other durable outcome. We are pragmatists as well as principled partners. We are prepared to help modernize the 1960 security architecture through a new treaty, to advance confidence-building measures, and to work Türkiye's European path constructively by keeping it from being held hostage to this dispute. We align closely with the United Kingdom, the Special Adviser, and the European Union, and we urge every delegation to negotiate in good faith toward a comprehensive, Cypriot-led settlement.

## ROUND 2

**Republic of Cyprus**: To the TRNC and Türkiye: 'sovereign equality' and 'virgin birth' remain firm red lines. Resolution 541 is not negotiable, and a strong central government under a popularly elected President stands. But we bargain in good faith. Concrete package: if Türkiye commits to a verified, timelined withdrawal of foreign troops and settlers, Cyprus will lift its blocks on Türkiye's EU accession and the Direct Trade Regulation, and open Famagusta port once dispossessed Greek Cypriot owners reinstate property or are compensated. Second linkage: we will accept a Senate-elected Vice-President in exchange for locking in the popular-vote Presidency, plus a 10-year residency threshold for local voting. All contingent on a UN-supervised whole-island census by an independent agency. We thank Greece, the United States, the United Kingdom, and the EU for anchoring one Cyprus in law. The UK's cross-voting idea merits exploration within a single sovereignty.

**Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus**: We hear the United Kingdom and United States. To the UK: your UN-framed replacement of the 1960 treaties can work, but only as a Turkiye-Greece-Cyprus mechanism that preserves our 1960-level security. So: if that guarantee is genuinely retained, then we will engage seriously on modernizing the architecture. To Cyprus and Greece: 'popular-vote presidency' and a strong federal center remain red lines. A majority ticket disenfranchises Turkish Cypriots, so we hold to a Senate-elected, community-rotating presidency. Now a concrete package: if the US and UK deliver Direct Trade Regulation implementation and Ankara Protocol movement, then we will open Famagusta Port under UN or EU supervision and table generous Varosha redevelopment terms for Greek Cypriot owners. Security-for-trade is our linkage. We coordinate fully with Turkiye and welcome the UK and US as honest brokers of these confidence measures.

**Greece**: Greece hears Türkiye and the TRNC, but 'sovereign equality' and a 'virgin birth' remain a hard red line: UNSC 541 is settled law and no reborn two-state entity is negotiable. Yet we bargain in good faith. To Türkiye directly: if you commit to a defined-timeline withdrawal of forces and replacement of the 1960 guarantee with a modern UN-framed treaty among a united Cyprus, Greece, and Türkiye, then Greece will actively champion de-linking your EU accession path and easing Direct Trade and Ankara Protocol restrictions, as the UK and US have signaled. That is a real side-payment. On governance, we can accept a Senate-elected Vice-President in exchange for a directly elected President, no rotation. On Famagusta: reopen Varosha to its lawful owners under EU/UN supervision, and we support opening the port. We stand with Cyprus, and welcome US, UK partnership.

**Turkey**: We thank the United Kingdom and United States for offering to de-link Türkiye's European path from this dispute and to modernize the 1960 architecture. Let us make it concrete. If London and Washington deliver the Direct Trade Regulation and open Famagusta port under EU/UN supervision, then, coordinating with the TRNC, we will table generous resettlement and redevelopment terms for Greek Cypriots in Varosha. Further: if a new UN-framed treaty preserves genuine Turkish involvement and safeguards, we will support replacing the old guarantees, as the UK and US propose. But to Greece and the Republic of Cyprus, hear our red line plainly: there will be no abrogation of the Treaty of Guarantee without an acceptable replacement, and no strong central government that disenfranchises Turkish Cypriots. Equality first, then confidence measures. We stand with the TRNC.

**United Kingdom**: With the United States, the SASG, and the Republic of Cyprus, the UK builds this coalition around one indivisible anchor: single sovereignty, single citizenship, single international personality. On that our red line holds, so to Turkiye and the TRNC, 'virgin birth' and two sovereign states are non-starters. But we hear your isolation concern, and we deal. Here is a package: if Turkiye and the TRNC accept a single-personality federation and a UN-framed replacement for the 1960 guarantee treaties, the UK will actively champion opening Famagusta port and Ercan airport under EU supervision, press to unblock the Direct Trade Regulation, and de-link Turkiye's EU accession from this dispute, representing that case in Brussels. To Cyprus and Greece: in exchange we ask flexibility on weighted or cross-voting so both communities feel secure. On Varosha, compensate lawful owners while crediting present investment. Reciprocity for reciprocity.

**United States**: To Türkiye and the TRNC: we hear your two demands, ending isolation and security. Here is a package. If you drop 'sovereign equality' and two-state 'virgin birth' and accept a single international personality, then the United States will actively champion opening Famagusta and recognizing Ercan under EU/UN supervision, implementation of the Direct Trade Regulation, and de-linking Cyprus from Türkiye's EU accession so those talks restart. That linkage is real leverage we will spend. On security, we back replacing the 1960 Treaties with a new tripartite treaty, with any residual intervention role confined to a constituent state, not a veto over the whole island. To Cyprus and Greece: to reassure Turkish Cypriots, we will support weighted/cross-voting and reasonable residency limits in the north. Our red line stands: no recognition of the TRNC, and permanent division is unacceptable. We coordinate with the United Kingdom, the SASG, and the EU.

## ROUND 3

**Republic of Cyprus**: Mr. Special Adviser, we close with measured optimism. Real convergence emerged: the UK, US, Greece, and EU all affirm a single sovereignty, single citizenship, and single international personality, and support de-linking Türkiye's EU path and unblocking direct trade against genuine confidence-building. We can support a Senate-elected Vice-President, a 10-year residency threshold for local voting, cross-voting mechanisms within one Republic, and a UN-framed treaty replacing the 1960 guarantees. Our red lines held intact: no recognition of the 'TRNC', no 'virgin birth', no rotating presidency, no external right of intervention, no permanent limits on Greek Cypriots. Gaps persist on political equality, the central government's strength, and troop withdrawal. Way forward: a UN-supervised whole-island census by an independent agency, a sequenced timeline binding verified troop withdrawal to our EU concessions, and Varosha's return to lawful owners. We thank our partners.

**Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus**: Progress is real where confidence measures meet security. We can support the emerging package: opening Famagusta Port under UN or EU supervision, implementing the Direct Trade Regulation and Ankara Protocol movement, and de-linking Turkiye's European path, welcomed from the UK and US. We can also support a UN-framed treaty replacing the 1960 arrangements, but only as a Turkiye-Greece-Cyprus mechanism preserving our 1960-level security. Our red lines held and remain: no strong central government, no popular-vote presidency that disenfranchises Turkish Cypriots, no abrogation of the Guarantee without an acceptable replacement, and any census conducted by our own authorities. The gap is structural equality; Nicosia's single-personality demand does not yet meet it. Way forward: a UN-supervised working group to sequence trade-for-security steps first, then table a two-founding-states architecture. We coordinate fully with Turkiye.

**Greece**: Mr. Special Adviser, Greece leaves this plenary encouraged that real convergence emerged. We can support several understandings: replacing the 1960 guarantees with a modern UN-framed treaty among a united Cyprus, Greece, and Turkiye; reopening Varosha to its lawful owners and Famagusta port under EU/UN supervision; easing Direct Trade and Ankara Protocol restrictions; and, as genuine confidence-building, de-linking Turkiye's European path. On governance we hold the compromise: a directly elected President with a Senate-elected Vice-President. Our red lines held firmly: no recognition of the entity in the north, no 'virgin birth' or two-state sovereign equality, no confederation with a hollow center, and no outside right of military intervention over the island. Gaps persist on single international personality and a timelined withdrawal. Way forward: task the SASG to draft the tripartite treaty and commission an independent island-wide census, sequencing confidence measures against verified troop drawdown.

**Turkey**: Mr. Special Adviser, this process has produced real convergence on confidence measures, and Türkiye's red lines held. We welcome the emerging consensus, from the UK, US, and even Greece, to modernize the 1960 architecture through a new tripartite treaty, and we can support it provided genuine Turkish involvement and safeguards remain, never a bare abrogation. We can support de-linking Türkiye's EU path, Direct Trade Regulation, opening Famagusta and Ercan under EU/UN supervision, and, coordinating with the TRNC, generous Varosha redevelopment for Greek Cypriot owners. But gaps remain fundamental: political equality is unmet while Nicosia insists on a strong center and a popular-vote presidency, which we reject, as we reject any outside census or forced repatriation. Way forward: sequence equality first, then implement the trade-and-security package in parallel, all coordinated with the TRNC. Equality is the price of a settlement.

**United Kingdom**: Mr. Special Adviser, real ground has been gained. A workable core is emerging: replacing the 1960 guarantee with a modern UN-framed treaty among a united Cyprus, Greece and Turkiye; opening Famagusta and Ercan under EU/UN supervision; unblocking Direct Trade; and de-linking Turkiye's EU path, which the UK will champion in Brussels. I welcome Cyprus's and Greece's openness to a Senate-elected Vice-President, verified census, and cross-voting to reassure Turkish Cypriots. The UK's red line held throughout: single sovereignty, single citizenship, single international personality, no recognition of two sovereign states, and no bypassing of the SASG. The unresolved gap is the presidency and 'virgin birth' framing. My proposed way forward: mandate the SASG to convene technical working groups to bank the agreed confidence-building measures now, while an executive-formula compromise is drafted, so momentum is not lost. The UK stands ready to underpin every step.

**United States**: Three rounds in, real convergence has emerged. A working understanding is within reach: replacing the 1960 Treaties with a new UN-framed treaty among a united Cyprus, Greece, and Türkiye, with any residual intervention confined to a constituent state, not a veto over the island. On confidence-building, there is broad support for opening Famagusta and Ercan under EU/UN supervision, implementing the Direct Trade Regulation, and de-linking Türkiye's EU accession, all of which the United States will actively champion. On governance, weighted and cross-voting plus reasonable residency limits are gaining traction. The gap that persists is sovereignty: 'sovereign equality' and two-state 'virgin birth' remain unresolved, and our red line held, no recognition of the TRNC and no acceptance of permanent division. Way forward: task the SASG to convert these understandings into a sequenced, verifiable roadmap, security-for-trade first, sovereignty framed as a single personality.
