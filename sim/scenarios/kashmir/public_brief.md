# Jammu and Kashmir: UNSCR 2900 Peace Conference

## Current situation

The former princely state of Jammu and Kashmir has been divided since 1947. India administers the Kashmir Valley, Jammu and Ladakh; Pakistan administers Azad Kashmir and Gilgit-Baltistan; China holds Aksai Chin and the Shaksam Valley. The Line of Control has been the de facto boundary since 1972, and the plebiscite called for by UN Security Council Resolution 47 in 1948 — conditioned on troop withdrawals that never occurred — has never been held. In August 2019 India revoked the special status of Jammu and Kashmir under Article 370 and reorganized it into Union Territories under direct rule; its Supreme Court upheld the revocation in December 2023. In April 2025 an attack at Pahalgam killed 26 people; India suspended its participation in the Indus Waters Treaty, and between 7 and 10 May India and Pakistan exchanged missile and drone strikes across their de facto border before agreeing a ceasefire. Roughly 950,000 people remain displaced within the region and across its borders, many in camps to which the Indian Army restricts access. Under UNSCR 2900 the Security Council has authorized the Secretary-General to convene a multilateral peace conference on the territory and governance of the old princely state. Delegations from India, Pakistan, China, Russia, the United Kingdom and the United States convene in Geneva, joined — unprecedentedly — by a delegation from Jammu and Kashmir itself, facilitated by a UN Special Representative.

## Negotiation chapters on the table

**Chapter I — Territory.** Sovereignty over the former princely state; whether the Line of Control becomes an international border; Azad Kashmir and Gilgit-Baltistan; India's reorganization of the territory; and the Sino-Indian claims to Aksai Chin, the Shaksam Valley and Arunachal Pradesh.

**Chapter II — Governance.** The end-state for Jammu and Kashmir — independence, autonomy, or local self-government; the status of Article 370 and Article 356; the terms of any plebiscite, including whether autonomy appears on the ballot, who administers it, and the troop withdrawals UNSCR 47 requires; and who provides security.

**Chapter III — Human Rights and Displaced Persons.** Whether human rights violations are investigated internationally or domestically; the status, protection and repatriation of some 950,000 displaced people; access to the camps and to the Kashmir Valley; and the screening of displaced populations against militant infiltration.

Raised at the table but outside the chapters: state support for militant organizations, and the Indian and Pakistani nuclear weapons programs.

## Procedure & rules

Sessions convene in plenary under a UN Special Representative appointed by the Secretary-General, who facilitates but does not arbitrate. Opening and closing statements are on the record. Ad hoc bilateral and multilateral sessions are arranged through the control group and occur only if every invited party agrees to attend — several delegations arrive with standing instructions restricting which counterparts they may meet, and in which formats. Each delegation must protect its privileged instructions and may not disclose another party's confidential positions. Conduct in common or public areas is subject to intelligence collection.
